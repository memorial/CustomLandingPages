What is obesity disease?

To be overweight doesn’t not only a cosmetic concern but also obesity is a disease that results from a combination of inherited factors, combined with the environment and personal diet and exercise choices. Obesity disease raises weight-related health problems such as diabetes, high blood pressure, heart failure and certain cancers. Obesity disease is diagnosed when your body mass index (BMI) is 30 or higher.

What are the obesity health problems?

There are many health problems related to obesity. Unhealthy nutrients can increase your weight and your "bad" LDL cholesterol, so that obesity can cause cardiovascular disease (mainly heart disease and stroke), type 2 diabetes, musculoskeletal disorders like osteoarthritis, and some cancers (endometrial, breast and colon). Carrying extra fat (over weight) contributes asthma symptoms which lead to restrictive pulmonary damage because of failure of muscles of breathing. Overweight and obesity and their associated health problems are the globally problem which is the reason for premature death and substantial disability.

How much obesity surgery / bariatric surgery cost?

Mostly, the success of Bariatric surgery is usually described as achieving a 50% loss or more excess body weight and keep it for at least 5 years. So the cost of Bariatric surgery success is not related to success of weight loss. To determine cost of weight loss surgery depends on many factors such as type of surgery, the bariatric surgeon you want to be performed, hospital facility, location etc.

What is the gastric sleeve surgery (gastric by pass)?

Vertical sleeve gastrectomy (the gastric sleeve surgery), which helps you to lose weight, is a bariatric surgery that eliminates 75-80% of the stomach. Two treatment options for obesity involve gastric sleeve surgery (Roux-en-Y gastric bypass) or non-surgical obesity treatment which approaches comprising behavioral therapy. Compared with noninvasive treatment of obesity, gastric sleeve surgery leads to greater body weight loss and higher remission rates of type 2 diabetes and metabolic syndrome.
